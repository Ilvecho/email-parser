The day Elon Musk tried to buy OpenAI for just shy of $100B…

Things over at xAI headquarters must not be going so smoothly training Grok 3, because Elon Musk just submitted a $97.6B bid to purchase his main rival, OpenAI. 

You’d think overhauling the government would be keeping him busy enough…

Here’s the deets on the deal:

  • xAI (Elon’s AI company) and a bunch of investment funds made the offer.

  • The bid is for the non-profit that controls OpenAI.

  • If it happens, xAI could merge with OpenAI.

In case you forgot, OpenAI is still a non-profit—but it’s trying to become for-profit by end of 2026 (and raise a new round of $40B ASAP).

OpenAI’s board now must consider this offer as it attempts to value itself—and Elon’s crew is prepared to match or beat any counter offers.

We didn’t go to business school, but we watched A LOT of Succession… is this the dreaded “bear hug”? Feels like it!

Quick history lesson—for those of you who never read The Founders, this move is ripped from the PayPal Mafia playbook:

  • Elon’s original X.com company wanted to be the ultimate online bank.

  • It and Peter Thiel’s PayPal were fighting each other for online payment market share, and they were both losing tons of money.

  • Eventually, instead of going broke fighting a losing battle—they merged.

That same exact thing is happening right now, but in AI:

  • We talked about this on Sunday, but AI is facing a price war driven largely by DeepSeek—it’s why you now get hundreds of free o3-mini queries.

  • After the big models do all the heavy lifting, smaller AI models can pop up and bring the cost of intelligence waaay down.

  • Sam wrote about this yesterday, explaining how the cost of AI has dropped by 10x every 12 months (and 150x from 2023 to 2024, specifically).

What Sam didn’t write about is how this price war undercuts the economics of OpenAI’s infrastructure investments, which Sam says will require significantly more scale (and matching spend) to train the next level of intelligence.

And for every DeepSeek that pops out and lowers the prices OpenAI can charge, that’s less ROI Sam (and really, Sam’s investors) can make off all the NVIDIA chips they have to buy.

There’s one more thing, too: Not to spoil the ending of The Founders, but y’know what went down after that PayPal / X.com merger happened? Elon was out as CEO—replaced by Thiel—and now, the same thing could happen to Sam. Call it Elon’s ultimate revenge?
