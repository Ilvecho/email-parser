<h3>Anthropic: 80% of Production Code Now AI-Generated</h3>
<p>Anthropic revealed that Claude now authors more than 80% of production code merged into its codebase, with the average engineer merging 8x as much code per day compared to 2024. The company describes this as early evidence of recursive self-improvement, where AI systems help design and build more capable future AI systems. On open-ended coding tasks, Claude's success rate reached 76%, up 50 points in six months. In one internal test, Claude Mythos Preview accelerated model-training code by ~52x, compared with ~3x for Claude Opus 4 in May 2025. When humans took wrong research directions, Mythos suggested better next steps 64% of the time. Anthropic emphasizes that humans still maintain control over direction-setting and judgment, while AI handles more execution tasks like writing code, running experiments, and debugging. The company outlined three possible futures: progress stalls, AI labs gain efficiency while humans stay in charge, or AI systems become capable of fully building their successors. [1]</p>

<h2>Prompt Tip of the Day</h2>
<p>Today's skill is to make your chatbot produce a "work receipt" after any important task. The goal is simple: measure finished output, review required, time saved, and risk. That keeps you from confusing activity with value.</p>

<p>After a project, paste this prompt into ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini. Then compare the AI's claims against what you actually shipped.</p>

<p><strong>Review the work we just completed and create an AI work receipt.</strong></p>

<p><strong>Include:</strong><br>
1. Finished output: What was actually completed?<br>
2. Human baseline: How long would this likely take me manually?<br>
3. AI-assisted time: How long did this take with you?<br>
4. Review required: What did I still need to check, rewrite, or fix?<br>
5. Risk: What could be wrong, incomplete, or misleading?<br>
6. Final value estimate: Was this a small assist, a major time saver, or not worth using AI for?</p>

<p><strong>Be conservative. Do not count drafts, ideas, or unused output as completed work.</strong></p>

<p>The key line is "be conservative." AI is great at sounding productive. The receipt makes it prove the work survived contact with reality. [1]</p>
