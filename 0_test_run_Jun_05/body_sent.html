Anthropic: 80% of Production Code Now AI-Generated

Anthropic revealed that Claude now authors more than 80% of production code merged into its codebase, with the average engineer merging 8x as much code per day compared to 2024. The company describes this as early evidence of recursive self-improvement, where AI systems help design and build more capable future AI systems. On open-ended coding tasks, Claude's success rate reached 76%, up 50 points in six months. In one internal test, Claude Mythos Preview accelerated model-training code by ~52x, compared with ~3x for Claude Opus 4 in May 2025. When humans took wrong research directions, Mythos suggested better next steps 64% of the time. Anthropic emphasizes that humans still maintain control over direction-setting and judgment, while AI handles more execution tasks like writing code, running experiments, and debugging. The company outlined three possible futures: progress stalls, AI labs gain efficiency while humans stay in charge, or AI systems become capable of fully building their successors. [1]

Cognition Introduces $10M AI Productivity Guarantee

Cognition launched an AI Productivity Guarantee for enterprise Devin customers, promising to fund usage up to $10M if Devin delivers less engineering value than customers pay for. The company's measurement system checks whether Devin's work was useful and estimates how long a human engineer would have taken to complete the same job. This approach directly addresses corporate AI's measurement problem, moving beyond typical metrics like tokens burned or messages sent to focus on actual time saved. [1]

Prompt Tip of the Day

Today's skill is to make your chatbot produce a "work receipt" after any important task. The goal is simple: measure finished output, review required, time saved, and risk. That keeps you from confusing activity with value.

After a project, paste this prompt into ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini. Then compare the AI's claims against what you actually shipped.

Review the work we just completed and create an AI work receipt.

Include:
1. Finished output: What was actually completed?
2. Human baseline: How long would this likely take me manually?
3. AI-assisted time: How long did this take with you?
4. Review required: What did I still need to check, rewrite, or fix?
5. Risk: What could be wrong, incomplete, or misleading?
6. Final value estimate: Was this a small assist, a major time saver, or not worth using AI for?

Be conservative. Do not count drafts, ideas, or unused output as completed work.

The key line is "be conservative." AI is great at sounding productive. The receipt makes it prove the work survived contact with reality. [1]
