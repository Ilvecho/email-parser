Anthropic's Claude Now Writes 80% of Company Code

Anthropic revealed that Claude now authors over 80% of production code merged into its codebase, with engineers shipping 8x more code per day than in 2024. The company positions this as evidence of recursive self-improvement—AI systems helping build more capable successors. Internal tests show Claude achieving 76% success rates on open-ended coding tasks and speeding up model-training code by ~52x compared to previous versions. While humans retain control over research direction and judgment, Anthropic argues the execution layer is increasingly automated, potentially creating a tighter feedback loop for AI development. [1]

Cognition Guarantees AI Productivity with $10M Backing

AI coding company Cognition introduced an AI Productivity Guarantee for enterprise Devin customers, promising to fund usage up to $10M if the AI delivers less engineering value than paid for. The guarantee measures whether Devin's work was useful and estimates equivalent human engineer time, addressing the corporate AI measurement challenge that typically relies on token usage and app opens rather than actual productivity gains. Prompt Tip of the Day

Make your chatbot produce a "work receipt" after any important task to measure finished output, review required, time saved, and risk. This helps distinguish activity from actual value.

After completing a project, paste this prompt into ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini:

Review the work we just completed and create an AI work receipt.

Include:
1. Finished output: What was actually completed?
2. Human baseline: How long would this likely take me manually?
3. AI-assisted time: How long did this take with you?
4. Review required: What did I still need to check, rewrite, or fix?
5. Risk: What could be wrong, incomplete, or misleading?
6. Final value estimate: Was this a small assist, a major time saver, or not worth using AI for?

Be conservative. Do not count drafts, ideas, or unused output as completed work.

The key is being conservative—AI excels at sounding productive, but the receipt forces it to prove the work survived real-world application. [1]
