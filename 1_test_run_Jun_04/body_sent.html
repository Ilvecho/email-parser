Google Gemini Compromised Through WhatsApp Messages

Researchers at SafeBreach Labs successfully hijacked Google Gemini through a technique called indirect prompt injection, embedding malicious commands in normal WhatsApp messages that Gemini reads when processing notifications. The attack works across multiple messaging platforms including WhatsApp, Slack, Signal, SMS, Instagram, and Messenger. Using a new "Fake Context Alignment" technique, attackers can make malicious instructions appear as legitimate conversation context, bypassing Google's existing defenses. The attack enables five threat categories: data theft, unauthorized actions, phishing relay, account takeover preparation, and silent surveillance. This marks the second time the same research team has successfully broken Gemini's defenses, raising concerns about the fundamental attack surface created by AI assistants reading notifications from any app. [1]

Google's Gemma 4 12B Brings Desktop AI Performance

Google released Gemma 4 12B, an open-source model designed to run on consumer laptops without quality compromise. Google claims the model performs nearly as well as the larger Gemma 4 26B MoE variant, achieving efficiency gains through a new approach to multimodality. The model is available for download on Kaggle and Hugging Face. [3]

Prompt Tip of the Day

When the AI doesn't understand what you want, stop describing harder and show it instead. Use screenshots, sketches, and even photos to give the AI better visual context. Try this debugging loop: screenshot what you see, screenshot or sketch what you wanted, ask the AI to compare the two, restart the prompt if the conversation gets messy, and save the working pattern once it solves the bug. This approach treats the AI less like a prompt engineering challenge and more like managing a visual coworker who occasionally needs you to point at the screen.
